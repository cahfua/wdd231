Dog Care Tips

Learn the essentials of keeping your dog healthy, safe, and well-loved in Samoa. Small daily habits make a big difference for our island dogs.

Food & Nutrition

Dogs need balanced meals with protein, vegetables, and clean water. Avoid feeding cooked bones, salty leftovers, or foods that are high in fat and spices.

  • •Feed 2 times a day for adults and 3–4 smaller meals for puppies.
  • •Always provide a bowl of fresh, clean water, especially in hot weather.
  • •Use a good-quality kibble or food that includes protein and vegetables.
  • •Never give chocolate, grapes, onions, or foods with xylitol (artificial sweetener).

Shelter & Comfort

Samoan sun and rain can be harsh. Even outdoor dogs need a safe spot where they can stay dry, cool, and protected from other animals.

  • •Provide a raised, dry sleeping area with shade from the sun.
  • •Use old blankets or mats that can be washed regularly to keep bedding clean.
  • •Make sure your dog has a secure yard or tether that does not tangle or choke.
  • •Give your dog safe chew toys so they don’t chew rubbish or dangerous objects.

Health & Safety

Regular checkups and simple prevention steps can protect your dog from many common illnesses and injuries.

  • •Keep vaccinations up to date (parvo, distemper, and rabies where required).
  • •Use deworming medicine every 3 months, or as recommended by your vet.
  • •Check paws and skin weekly for cuts, infections, or signs of discomfort.
  • •Keep rubbish, chemicals, and sharp objects out of reach.

Flea & Tick Prevention

Fleas and ticks are common in warm, humid climates like Samoa and can spread disease or cause severe itching.

  • •Use flea and tick treatments monthly (shampoo, spot-on, or vet-recommended tablets).
  • •Wash bedding often in hot water and dry in the sun to kill parasites.
  • •Keep grass and bushes trimmed where your dog spends time.
  • •See a vet if you notice hair loss, red skin, or constant scratching.

Spay & Neuter

Spaying or neutering prevents unwanted puppies and helps reduce the number of stray dogs in Samoa. It can also improve your dog’s long-term health and behavior.

  • •Talk with a local vet or shelter about low-cost spay/neuter options.
  • •Keep your dog indoors or supervised until they are fully healed after surgery.

When to See a Vet Immediately

Contact a veterinarian or local shelter right away if you notice any of these emergency signs:

  • •Difficulty breathing or collapsing.
  • •Not eating or drinking for more than 24 hours.
  • •Severe vomiting or diarrhea, especially in puppies.
  • •Serious injuries, deep cuts, or being hit by a car.
